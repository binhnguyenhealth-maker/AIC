Privacy policy

Effective July 12, 2026

This policy describes the AIC Chicago iOS app.

Location and scans

Your scan point is processed on your device against a bundled Chicago data pack. AIC does not send normal scan coordinates, manual pins, addresses, routes, geographic cells, or scan history to the AIC account service. AIC does not request background location. You may deny location permission and use the manual-pin alternative. Your U.S./metric display preference is stored only on your iPhone.

Cooked Score

Cooked Score is a historical data index that compares reported-incident concentration around this location with eligible Chicago comparison locations. It is not a live safety assessment or personal-risk prediction. The method uses privacy-coarsened, nearest-five category bands in non-overlapping cells and displays an estimated count, not an exact incident count. It is not a claim about causality or a guarantee of safety. The app displays the data source and source-through date.

Receipts and sharing

Cooked Receipts are rendered on your device. Exact coordinates, pins, addresses, routes, and exact timestamps are excluded. You control whether your public username and approximate neighborhood appear. The iOS share sheet shows the destinations you choose; cancelling it sends nothing from AIC. AIC does not operate a hosted receipt-card service.

Account data

You can scan and generate a report without an account. If you choose Sign in with Apple, AIC requests no Apple name or email. AIC stores a keyed hash of the Apple account identifier, an encrypted Apple refresh credential, an internal account identifier, your chosen public username, account status, and security/session records needed to sign you in. The account service does not need or accept scan locations.

Service providers

Apple provides Sign in with Apple and the App Store. Cloudflare hosts the minimal account and informational services. These providers may process technical request information under their own policies. AIC does not sell personal information or use cross-app advertising trackers.

Deletion and retention

You can delete your account in the app. After fresh Apple reauthentication and confirmation, AIC permanently removes the account, public username, Apple credential, deletion proof, and every AIC session. A keyed, non-public deletion marker is retained for 24 hours only to prevent a stale Apple response from recreating the deleted account.

AIC also asks Apple to revoke its authorizations. When Apple replaces an authorization token during reauthentication, or if Apple is temporarily unavailable during deletion, encrypted tokens may be queued under random, account-unlinked identifiers for bounded revocation retries. The queue contains no account ID, username, or scan location; each encrypted token is destroyed after success or after 20 attempts or 30 days, whichever comes first. Local account deletion still completes during an Apple outage. Deleting the app removes app-owned local state and temporary receipts; it cannot remove copies you previously saved or shared elsewhere.

Security and changes

AIC uses short-lived sessions and avoids logging authentication tokens. No system can be guaranteed perfectly secure. Material policy changes will be posted here with a new effective date.

Questions

Use the public AIC support issue tracker for questions; do not post exact locations, account identifiers, authentication data, or other personal information. Account deletion is available inside the app. See the support page for steps.
