Privacy policy

Effective July 12, 2026

This policy describes the AIC Chicago iOS app.

Location and scans

Your scan point is processed on your device against a bundled Chicago data pack. AIC does not send normal scan coordinates, manual pins, addresses, routes, geographic cells, or scan history to AIC. AIC does not request background location. You may deny location permission and use the manual-pin alternative. Your U.S./metric display preference is stored only on your iPhone.

Cooked Score

Cooked Score is a historical data index that compares reported-incident concentration around this location with eligible Chicago comparison locations. It is not a live safety assessment or personal-risk prediction. The method uses privacy-coarsened, nearest-five category bands in non-overlapping cells and displays an estimated count, not an exact incident count. It is not a claim about causality or a guarantee of safety. The app displays the data source and source-through date.

Receipts and sharing

Cooked Receipts are rendered on your device. Exact coordinates, pins, addresses, routes, and exact timestamps are excluded. You control whether the approximate neighborhood appears. The iOS share sheet shows the destinations you choose; cancelling it sends nothing from AIC. AIC does not operate a hosted receipt-card service.

No accounts

AIC v1 has no account, sign-in, public-username, or hosted-content feature. AIC does not collect an account identifier, name, email address, authentication credential, or session record.

Service providers

Apple provides the App Store and iOS services such as the user-invoked share sheet. Cloudflare hosts these informational policy and support pages and the fixed global signed pack-status file described below. These providers may process technical request information under their own policies. AIC does not sell personal information or use cross-app advertising trackers.

Emergency pack-status check

On launch and when returning to the foreground, subject to a 15-minute rate limit, AIC may fetch one fixed global signed pack-status file so a materially flawed bundled pack can be withdrawn. The request contains no city, coordinates, scan data, account or device identifier, installed-pack identifier, query, or body. Cloudflare receives the request IP address and ordinary HTTP/TLS or security metadata and may retain that information under its policies to deliver, protect, and operate the service. AIC disables developer-accessible Workers Logs and Logpush and uses no cookies, analytics, advertising identifiers, or personalization for this check. AIC conservatively discloses this provider metadata as linked Other Data used for app functionality, never tracking. Pressing Scan does not trigger a status request.

Deletion and retention

Because v1 has no account, AIC has no account data to delete or retain. Cloudflare may retain the limited request and network-security metadata described above according to its policies; AIC does not receive a user-level Workers log. Deleting the app removes its local preferences and temporary receipts; it cannot remove provider network records or copies you previously saved or shared elsewhere.

Security and changes

No system can be guaranteed perfectly secure. Material policy changes will be posted here with a new effective date.

Questions

Email admin@holdmetoit.info for private support; do not send exact locations or other sensitive personal information. See the support page for help.
